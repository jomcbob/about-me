About me

My name is Andrew Borlin. I am a 16 years old and have been in 4-H for four years. This year I am doing a rabbit project and a Turkey project. My goal is to be a full stack web develper

4-H teaches real skills that actually matter. You learn how to manage time, handle responsibility, and follow through on long-term projects. It's not just about raising animals—it's about learning how to work, solve problems, and build something from the ground up.


Being in 4-H has helped me grow by giving me hands-on experience. I’ve had to budget, stay on schedule, and take care of animals. I’ve also learned how to communicate with adults, present my work, and stay focused when things don’t go right. These are the kinds of lessons that stick with you and actually prepare you for life.


The financial side of these projects is real. Feed, vet bills, supplies, and entry fees aren’t cheap. Donations help cover those costs, and they also allow me to take my projects further—whether that’s upgrading my setup, competing at bigger events, or saving for future goals like college or a trade.


If you're thinking about donating, you're not just throwing money at a hobby. You're investing in someone who's putting in the work and learning what it takes to succeed. It helps now, and it will help me pay for the next phase in my learning.
